GRADE 11 ABM SUBJECTS

Reading and Writing Skills

Reading and writing teaches essential skills for understanding, analyzing, and producing various types of texts, focusing on critical thinking, different writing patterns (narration, description, persuasion, etc.), organizing information using tools like graphic organizers, and connecting reading to writing for academic and professional success. It bridges decoding and comprehension with written expression, showing how reading informs writing and vice versa.

Statistics and Probability

Statistics and Probability is a core mathematics subject dealing with collecting, analyzing, interpreting, and displaying data (Statistics) and the study of chance and likelihood of events (Probability), forming a crucial tool for decision-making, modeling uncertainty, and understanding patterns in science, finance, and daily life, covering topics from random variables and distributions to hypothesis testing and regression. It teaches how to reason from samples to populations (Statistics) and populations to samples (Probability) using concepts like normal distribution, sampling, and correlation.

Pagbasa at Pagsusuri ng Iba't Ibang Teksto Tungo sa Pananaliksik

It focuses on reading, understanding, and analyzing various types of texts to build the skills students need to conduct systematic research and produce basic research writing.

Chemistry 2

It builds on the fundamentals introduced in Chemistry 1 and focuses on more advanced chemical concepts, problem-solving, and applications in real-world and scientific settings

Basic Calculus

Basic Calculus is the study of continuous change, built on the foundation of limits, and divided into two main branches: Differential Calculus (rates of change, slopes, derivatives) and Integral Calculus (accumulation, areas, integrals), linked by the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, essential for science, engineering, and economics. Key topics include limits, continuity, differentiation rules, integration techniques, and applications like optimization

Practical Research

Practical Research teach critical thinking and problem-solving through hands-on research, focusing on understanding phenomena (PR1) and testing hypotheses (PR2) for real-world application, moving from inquiry to investigation and immersion.

Empowerment Technologies (ETECH)

ETECH focuses on digital literacy, online safety, research, and practical skills with web platforms (like social media, cloud) and apps (image/video editing) to become tech-savvy individuals for academic and professional life.

Disaster Readiness and Risk Reduction (DRRR)

Disaster Readiness and Risk Reduction (DRRR) focuses on understanding disasters, hazards (like earthquakes, typhoons, floods), and developing practical skills for preparedness, mitigation, and response, teaching students to assess risks, create emergency plans, and apply scientific knowledge to everyday life, crucial for disaster-prone regions like the Philippines. The curriculum covers hazards, vulnerability, exposure, risk factors, effects on people, and government policies, aiming to build resilient individuals and communities.
